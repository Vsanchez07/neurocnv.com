Parkinson's disease is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder that primarily affects the brain's dopamine-producing neurons. This leads to a decline in dopamine levels, causing a range of symptoms including tremors, slow movement, and stiffness. While there is no cure, various treatments can help manage the symptoms and improve quality of life.

Symptoms:

Motor Symptoms:

  • Tremor: Shaking, often starting in one hand or arm, and more noticeable when the limb is relaxed.
  • Slow Movement (Bradykinesia): Physical movements are slower than normal, making everyday tasks difficult.
  • Rigidity (Stiffness): Stiffness and tension in the muscles, making movement and facial expressions difficult.
  • Postural Instability: Difficulty maintaining balance and posture, increasing the risk of falls.

Non-Motor Symptoms:

  • Depression and Anxiety: Mood changes and emotional difficulties are common.
  • Sleep Problems: Difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, or experiencing restful sleep.
  • Cognitive Difficulties: Memory and thinking problems can occur.
  • Loss of Smell: educed sense of smell is a common symptom.
  • Fatigue: Feeling tired and lacking energy.
